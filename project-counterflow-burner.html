Project Overview
85%
reduction in fuel consumption achieved through first-principles combustion redesign and precision miniaturisation

This project stands as one of the clearest demonstrations of first-principles thinking in Jamal's portfolio. Rather than incrementally improving the existing counter-flow burner, the fundamental question was posed: does the large-scale architecture remain valid at the required output level? The answer—no—led to a complete redesign from combustion physics upward.

Design Approach

The counter-flow burner operates by introducing fuel and oxidiser in opposing streams, creating a stable flame zone between them. At reduced scale, the original geometry created excessive heat loss to surface area relative to the combustion volume, making the original proportions thermodynamically wasteful.

"The 85% saving wasn't found by refining the old design—it was found by asking whether the old design was ever necessary at this scale."

By re-deriving the flame zone geometry from mass flow and reaction rate equations, a miniaturised architecture was arrived at that maintained thermal efficiency while dramatically reducing fuel throughput. CFD analysis validated the flow field and confirmed stable combustion across the operating range before committing to final geometry.

Outcome

The redesigned miniaturised burner achieved an 85% reduction in fuel consumption versus the baseline design, with equivalent thermal output maintained. The project delivered full CAD documentation, CFD simulation results, and a manufacturing-ready design package.
