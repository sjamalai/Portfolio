Project Overview

A comprehensive suspension system designed and modelled for heavy-duty truck applications, with structural integrity and load distribution as the primary design drivers. The system required balancing ride quality with the extreme static and dynamic loads characteristic of commercial trucking applications.

"Load distribution engineered from the ground up—every joint, arm, and bushing justified by the physics."

The design was approached through a first-principles structural analysis: establishing the load envelope first, then deriving geometry to distribute those loads efficiently into the chassis. Finite element analysis was used to validate stress concentrations and identify regions for material optimisation.

Design Approach

The suspension architecture was selected based on kinematic requirements for heavy-duty applications—prioritising durability and predictable load transfer over minimal unsprung mass. Control arm geometry was optimised for camber gain under compression to maintain tyre contact patch stability under full load.

All pivot points and bushing interfaces were dimensioned to accommodate realistic tolerance stack-up while maintaining acceptable handling characteristics throughout the design service life.

Outcome

The completed design package included full 3D assembly models, 2D manufacturing drawings with GD&T callouts, and FEA simulation results demonstrating compliance with the structural requirements across the full load spectrum.
