Documentation Philosophy

Technical drawings are the definitive language of engineering. A well-executed drawing eliminates ambiguity entirely: every dimension, tolerance, datum reference, and surface finish specification communicates a precise manufacturing intention that can be executed correctly the first time, anywhere in the world, by any qualified machinist or fabricator.

"A drawing that requires a phone call to interpret is an incomplete drawing."

Across four years and multiple industry sectors, Jamal has produced documentation packages ranging from single-component detail drawings to multi-sheet assembly documentation with complete Bills of Materials and exploded view guides.

Scope & Standards

All drawings are produced to the applicable international standard for the target manufacturing region—ISO 2768 for general tolerances, ASME Y14.5 for GD&T callouts, and client-specific drawing standards where specified. Third-angle projection is used as default, with first-angle applied where European manufacturing partners require it.

GD&T is applied functionally, not decoratively: tolerance values are derived from the actual functional requirements of each feature, with datum reference frames established from assembly function rather than drawing convenience.

Deliverable Format

Drawing packages are delivered in both native CAD format and locked PDF, with revision control applied from the first issue. All drawings include complete title blocks, material callouts, surface finish specifications, and manufacturing notes required for unambiguous production.
