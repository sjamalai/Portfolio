Project Overview

This project involved the full CAD design of a high-fidelity industrial turbine ignition system for Siemens. Every component was precision-modeled from first-principles tolerancing through to final photorealistic render, delivering a complete design package suitable for manufacturing handoff.

"Precision-modeled from component tolerances to final render—engineering rigour at every stage."

The ignition system required careful coordination between high-temperature material selection, igniter placement geometry, and the surrounding turbine casing architecture. Tolerance stack-up analysis was performed to ensure reliable spark delivery under operational thermal expansion.

Design Approach

Beginning from Siemens' performance requirements, the design was decomposed into three primary sub-assemblies: the igniter body, the mounting interface, and the high-voltage lead routing system. Each was modelled independently then integrated into the full assembly, with clearance studies run at both cold and operational temperatures.

GD&T callouts were applied throughout to communicate manufacturing intent unambiguously, with critical datum references established from the turbine casing centreline.

Outcome

The complete design package was delivered on schedule, encompassing 3D models, detailed 2D drawings with full GD&T annotation, and a render suite suitable for client presentation. The design met all Siemens engineering sign-off criteria without revision cycles.
